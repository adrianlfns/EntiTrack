1. PREMISES:
The Landlord hereby leases to the Tenant, and the Tenant hereby leases from the Landlord, the real property located at:
456 Ocean View Drive, Unit 2A, Coastal City, FL 33139
(hereinafter referred to as the "Premises"), together with all fixtures, appliances (including refrigerator, stove, dishwasher, microwave), and amenities (e.g., communal pool, fitness center access) as currently existing on the Premises.

2. TERM:
The term of this Agreement shall be for a period of twelve (12) months, commencing on August 1, 2025, and ending on July 31, 2026 (the "Term").

3. RENT:
The Tenant shall pay to the Landlord a monthly rent of Two Thousand Five Hundred Dollars and Zero Cents ($2,500.00), payable in advance on the 1st day of each calendar month. Rent shall be paid to Evergreen Property Management LLC at the address specified above, or as otherwise directed by the Landlord in writing. A late fee of $50.00 shall be assessed if rent is not received by the 5th day of the month.

4. SECURITY DEPOSIT:
The Tenant shall pay a security deposit to the Landlord in the amount of Two Thousand Five Hundred Dollars and Zero Cents ($2,500.00) upon the execution of this Agreement. This deposit shall be held by the Landlord in a separate non-interest-bearing account as required by Florida law. The security deposit will be returned to the Tenant within thirty (30) days after the termination of this Agreement, less any deductions for damages beyond normal wear and tear, unpaid rent, or cleaning fees.

5. UTILITIES:
The Tenant shall be responsible for paying for all utilities and services consumed on the Premises, including but not limited to electricity, water, sewer, internet, and cable television. The Landlord shall be responsible for waste removal and common area maintenance fees.

6. MAINTENANCE AND REPAIRS:
a. Tenant's Responsibilities: The Tenant shall keep the Premises in a clean, sanitary, and good condition and shall make all repairs necessitated by the Tenant's negligence or misuse. The Tenant shall promptly notify the Landlord of any needed repairs or maintenance issues.
b. Landlord's Responsibilities: The Landlord shall be responsible for maintaining the structural soundness of the Premises and for major repairs to plumbing, electrical, heating, and air conditioning systems, provided such repairs are not necessitated by the Tenant's negligence.

7. USE OF PREMISES:
The Premises shall be used solely as a private residential dwelling for the Tenant(s) named herein and their immediate family. No commercial activity shall be conducted on the Premises.

9. ALTERATIONS:
The Tenant shall not make any alterations, additions, or improvements to the Premises without the prior written consent of the Landlord.

10. ENTRY BY LANDLORD:
The Landlord may enter the Premises with reasonable notice (typically 24 hours) for purposes of inspection, repairs, maintenance, or showing the Premises to prospective tenants or purchasers. In case of emergency, the Landlord may enter without prior notice.

11. DEFAULT:
If the Tenant fails to pay rent when due, violates any term of this Agreement, or abandons the Premises, the Landlord may, after providing proper notice as required by law, terminate this Agreement and regain possession of the Premises.

12. GOVERNING LAW:
This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Florida.

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties and supersedes all prior agreements, understandings, and negotiations, whether written or oral, relating to the subject matter hereof.
